## Design Context

### Users

Musicians and songwriters — primarily the maker and fellow hobbyist players — reaching for
the tool mid-session to find more interesting chords for a progression they're writing.
They know the basics (chords, keys, "a I–IV–V") but lean on the app for the theory
(modal interchange and other substitutions). It's used **at the instrument**: phone or
tablet propped up while a guitar or keyboard is in hand. Sessions are short, exploratory,
and creative — tap a progression, hear/see options, try them, keep the good ones.

### Brand Personality

Calm, focused, editorial, quietly crafted. A songwriting notebook, not a flashy producer
app. Confident restraint with a musical warmth. The interface should feel like a
well-made physical tool or instrument — tactile and considered — rather than a SaaS
dashboard or a busy DAW.

### Aesthetic Direction

Carry forward the established identity from the original app: a **muted sage-green
palette** (`#83a085` primary, `#5b885e` deep) on a **cream "mat" frame**, with **large
serif display type** for the chord root notes (à la miller-banner) paired with a clean
**sans** for UI text and labels (à la Montserrat). A signature move: the canvas shifts
from a **light input state** to a **darker "results" state** so it's always obvious what
you typed versus what the app suggested. Motion is gentle and springy — chords settle into
place; nothing bounces gratuitously.

Anti-references: piano-key skeuomorphism, neon/"dark studio" producer aesthetics, busy
multi-panel DAW chrome, generic AI gradients (cyan/purple glow), corporate SaaS layouts.

### Design Principles

1. **Typography is the interface** — chords are set like editorial display type (numeral /
   big serif root / quality suffix / source label), not boxed widget cards.
2. **One focused screen** — a generous canvas plus a thumb-reachable bottom dock; settings,
   keys, and saved songs tuck into a drawer.
3. **Input vs. results legibility** — the light→dark background shift and labelling make
   the distinction between your progression and the app's suggestions instant.
4. **Musical feedback** — sound and motion confirm actions; pressing a chord should _feel
   and sound_ like playing one (Web Audio playback is core, not an add-on).
5. **Calm by default, delight on interaction** — a restrained surface that rewards touch
   with crafted micro-interactions, never visual noise.
